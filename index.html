CS284A Final Project

Title

Covid Spread Visualization

Summary

Summary: We’re attempting to create an interactive visualization of how Covid particles are physically spread through the air under different conditions. We’ll then potentially additionally do human crowd (2D boids) simulation on top of that to see how different conditions in people “pathing” (specifically separation) affect Covid spread.

Team members

Richard Wang, Jordan Wong, Yi-Chen Chen, Weiyu Feng

Context

We’ll be trying to make a physically accurate simulation of how Covid particles spread and ideally do it in real time so that users can interact with it by changing conditions (masks, wind, humidity, ventilation). This will require an efficient particle simulation algorithm, and especially so if we make it in 3D instead of 2D. Depending on how much time we have left, we’ll then add an additional layer of complexity by incorporating human crowds (2D boids) on top of the cough simulation. Although it may not be 100% accurate to real life, our final deliverable could be used as a tool to study/present the covid spread in different settings. The biggest challenge will likely be getting this all to run in real time at a reasonable frame rate. We’ll likely need to do some simplifications if we can’t figure out ways to optimize our algorithms.

Goal & Deliverable

What we plan to deliver
  • 2D Cough Particle Simulation
    • Measure framerate for different optimizations
  • Interactive 2D Cough Particle Simulation
    • With/without mask
    • wind
    • Air Temperature/humidity
    • Ventilation/no ventilation
  • Interactive 3D Cough Particle Simulation
What we hope to deliver
  • Interactive Human Crowd (2D Boids) Simulation
    • Separation
    • Alignment
    • Cohesion
    • Naive “Coloring/Labeling” of Covid Infected people
  • Interactive Human Crowd (2D Boids) Simulation + 3D Cough Particle Simulation
    • "Coloring/Labeling” of Covid infected people touched by particles
